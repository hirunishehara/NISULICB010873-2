-Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery-

The Turtle Hatchery is located on the seashore about 2 kilometers north of Hikkaduwa. The hatchery is a tiny and private research organization that strives to rescue endangered marine turtles by displaying turtle eggs and demonstrating turtle development. For an additional price, there are various locations where you can release newborn turtles into the huge ocean. The greatest time to visit and see the turtles is from March to April.


The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is a conservation project dedicated to protecting and preserving sea turtles, including endangered species like the Olive Ridley, Green, and Hawksbill turtles. Here, you can learn about the life cycle of turtles, their nesting habits, and the threats they face in the wild. the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery often conducts educational programs and awareness campaigns to educate visitors and locals about the importance of turtle conservation. They highlight the threats posed by human activities, such as pollution, habitat destruction, and illegal poaching, and promote responsible tourism practices to protect these magnificent creatures.
